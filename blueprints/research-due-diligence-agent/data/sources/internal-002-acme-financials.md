# Internal memo — Acme financial detail and unit economics

Source: data-room / finance pack
Doc type: internal
Date: 2025-Q1

Acme's annual recurring revenue (ARR) at the end of 2024 was 18 million US dollars.
Net revenue retention (NRR) over the trailing twelve months was 119 percent, driven
by expansion in existing accounts as their RAG workloads grew. Gross churn of logos
was 9 percent annually, which is healthy for the mid-market segment.

The company is not yet profitable. It burned 11 million US dollars of cash in 2024
and held 22 million in the bank at year end, implying roughly 24 months of runway at
the current burn rate. The blended customer acquisition cost (CAC) payback period is
about 14 months.

Two financial-services customers together account for 31 percent of ARR, a
concentration risk a buyer should weigh. The largest single customer is roughly 19
percent of ARR. Contracts with both anchor accounts renew in the second half of 2025.
